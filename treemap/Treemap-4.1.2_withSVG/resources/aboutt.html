Treemaps
Treemaps are a space-filling visualization for hierarchical and categorical data. Treemaps enable users to manipulate the size and color of the rectangular areas that represent leaf nodes in the tree, based on the attributes of the data. Treemaps are a useful tool in understanding data and recognizing unusual trends or patterns. Ben Shneiderman at the Human-Computer Interaction Laboratory (HCIL) of the University of Maryland developed them, during the 1990s. A summary of treemaps and their growing set of applications appear at: http://www.cs.umd.edu/hcil/treemap-history/. Treemaps and their applications are a continuing topic of research at the HCIL. More information can be found at http://www.cs.umd.edu/hcil/treemap.
